# April 30, 2026 Changes (Listed 10 experts)


## Experts Collected & Why

### 1. Aleyda Solis
**Why:** One of the clearest voices on international SEO strategy in the AI era. Her 10-step AI search optimization checklist and SEOFOMO newsletter are primary sources for how AI changes keyword research and content workflows at scale.

### 2. Barry Schwartz
**Why:** The most prolific real-time chronicler of Google algorithm updates. Essential for understanding how Google's stance on AI-generated content has evolved, including the Helpful Content Update and AI Overviews rollout.

### 3. Brian Dean
**Why:** Founder of Backlinko and creator of the Skyscraper Technique. His co-authored 912M blog post study (with BuzzSumo) provides the empirical foundation for why AI-generated content volume fails — 94% of content earns zero links.

### 4. Eric Enge
**Why:** Lead co-author of *The Art of SEO* and published a rigorous 62-query comparison of ChatGPT vs. Google search. His data (Google wins commercial and local; ChatGPT wins content gap analysis) grounds the AI vs. traditional search debate in real numbers.

### 5. Lily Ray
**Why:** The industry's most cited analyst of Google's Quality Rater Guidelines. She documented the introduction of "Experience" to E-E-A-T and consistently argues that GEO is rebranded SEO — AI citations come from the same content that ranks in Google.

### 6. Marie Haynes
**Why:** Forensic-level analyst of Google's internal ranking systems. Her framework covering Google's three-layer ranking process (traditional → RankEmbed BERT → user experiment fine-tuning) and the Google Glue system is the deepest technical source in this collection.

### 7. Matt Cutts
**Why:** Former Head of Google's Webspam team. Led Panda and Penguin. His 23 quality questions used to train Panda's classifier are the direct ancestor of today's E-E-A-T framework — foundational historical context for how AI quality signals evolved.

### 8. Nathan Gotch
**Why:** Practitioner-focused SEO with a clear methodology (SEO Success Pyramid). Covers how to integrate AI tools into content workflows while preserving E-E-A-T signals through human expertise — a practical counterweight to theory-heavy sources.

### 9. Neil Patel
**Why:** Runs the largest empirical experiments on AI vs. human content at scale. His 68-site, 744-article study (AI averaged 52 visitors/month vs. 283 for human-written) and hallucination study across 6 LLMs are the most-cited data points in the AI content debate.

### 10. Rand Fishkin
**Why:** Founder of SparkToro and the industry's leading data-driven skeptic of AI search hype. His SparkToro/Datos 2024 study (Google grew 20%+ in 2024; ChatGPT has 373x fewer searches) and GEO measurement skepticism provide the essential counternarrative.

### 11. Ahrefs (Channel)
**Why:** Their AEO Course series provides the clearest structured explanation of how AI search engines work, what AI visibility means, and how query fan-out changes content strategy. Direct, practitioner-level education content.

### 12. Matt Diggity
**Why:** Runs live SEO experiments and publishes the results. His videos on Reddit's role in AI search and hands-on AI-SEO campaign results provide real-world data that balances the more theory-driven expert sources.
